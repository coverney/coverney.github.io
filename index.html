Hello!
I’m Cassandra

A Scholar, builder, storyteller, and baker

I’m a first-year master's student at MIT Media Lab’s Center for Constructive Communication. I recently graduated from Olin College of Engineering with a degree in computer science. I am passionate about designing computer science curriculum for kids and doing societal computing research. Societal computing is the intersection of software engineering, empathy, and society. At the Media Lab, I aim to explore different areas of social network research and create tools that aid in computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW). Currently, I am especially interested in the use of storytelling in community governance. I’m super excited to begin my grad school adventure and design impactful technology that can bring different groups of people together.

Latest Work

Shared Mobility Visual Analytics Tool

I created a visual analytics tool that can help city planners manage their shared mobility services. The tool is a React web application that analyzes scooter-share event data, estimates spatial demand, and generates an interactive data visualization page where users can map out usage and demand.

Ruina Crash Reporting App

For my senior capstone project at Olin, I worked with four other students to create a mobile application, Ruina, that improves and standardizes the car crash reporting system. Ruina follows the Department of Transportation’s data collection guidelines, supports versatile data export formats, and includes features such as GPS mapping and barcode scanning to auto populate fields and speed up the experience.

Break Through

As part of my AHS (Art, Humanities, and Social Sciences) Capstone, I wrote a creative writing booklet centered around the theme of breaking through the shields we put up and the bubbles enclosing us. The stories range from creative nonfiction to magical realism. Each piece is matched with a writing prompt and some related recipes.
